Safe Investing Guidelines

Follow these fundamental principles to build long-term wealth safely

Invest Only Surplus Funds

Never invest money you need for daily expenses, emergencies, or short-term goals. Only invest surplus funds you can afford to lock away for 3-5 years.

Diversify Your Portfolio

Don't put all eggs in one basket. Spread investments across sectors, market caps, and asset classes to reduce risk.

Do Your Own Research

Never blindly follow tips. Understand the business, read annual reports, analyze financials, and make informed decisions.

Think Long-Term

Wealth creation takes time. Avoid the temptation of quick profits. Stay invested for at least 3-5 years to ride out market volatility.

Avoid Leverage & Margin

Never borrow money to invest. Leverage amplifies losses and can wipe out your capital. Invest only your own money.

Review Regularly

Monitor your portfolio quarterly. Rebalance if needed, but avoid frequent trading based on short-term market movements.

Trading Pitfalls to Avoid

Common mistakes that lead to losses - learn from others' experiences

Intraday Trading / F&O

95% of intraday traders lose money. Derivatives (F&O) are high-risk instruments designed for hedging, not speculation. Avoid unless you're an expert.

Following Unverified Tips

WhatsApp groups, Telegram channels, and social media "gurus" often promote pump-and-dump schemes. Always verify sources and do your own analysis.

Emotional Trading

Fear and greed drive poor decisions. Panic selling during crashes and FOMO buying during rallies destroy wealth. Stick to your investment plan.

Ignoring Risk Management

Not setting stop-losses, over-concentrating in one stock, or investing borrowed money can lead to catastrophic losses.

Chasing Penny Stocks

Low-priced stocks (<₹10) are often illiquid, manipulated, and fundamentally weak. Focus on quality businesses, not cheap prices.

Overtrading

Frequent buying and selling erodes returns through brokerage, taxes, and poor timing. Less is more - trade less, invest more.
